How does Light affect plant's growth?

What experiment are we doing?
  • Measuring the growth of kidney beans regarding the wavelength of light in which each plant is grown.
  • Kidney beans are short day plants (long night plants)
  • We have five different kinds of light.
  • There are red, white, green, blue and daylight.

What is our hypothesis?
  • We predict that red light will be the most effective for the plant to grow because...
    1. Plants reflect green light, making it green
    2. Plants absorb 430–450 nm (blue) and 640–680 nm (red)
    3. This means chlorophyll absorbs mainly blue and red light, while green light is reflected.
  • We predict that red light will be the most effective for the plant to grow because...

What if the plant was another colour?
  • If our plant was another colour, for example red cabbage
  • It would reflect red light (since it is red), and it would absorb green light more than it would absorb red light.
  • This means that red cabbage would grow better in green light than red light.

Overall, the colour of the plant affects which light colour is best for its growth. Different plants may have different optimal light colours based on their pigments and how they interact with light.
